**Introduction**

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are two fundamental types of Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs). While both can provide state-of-the-art performance in various applications, they differ significantly in terms of design and functionality.

**Key Differences**

#### Temporal vs Spatial

*   RNNs process sequential data, utilizing internal memory to keep track of temporal relationships between elements in a sequence (Stack Exchange Network).

In conclusion, while both RNNs and CNNs are powerful types of ANNs, they differ significantly in terms of design and functionality. RNNs are well-suited for NLP tasks and have an infinite impulse response, while CNNs are well-suited for computer vision tasks and have a finite impulse response. Understanding these key differences is crucial for selecting the appropriate type of ANN for a given application.
